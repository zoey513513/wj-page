About Me

I am a postdoctoral fellow in the Mooney Lab at Harvard University. My research explores how the mechanical and metabolic environments of tumors regulate disease progression, immunity, and therapeutic response.

I earned my Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from Vanderbilt University under Dr. Cynthia Reinhart-King, where I studied how diabetic hyperglycemia and extracellular-matrix stiffening influence tumor angiogenesis and immune regulation.

Research Overview

  • Diabetic hyperglycemia alters the immune microenvironment of tumors through ECM glycation.
  • Matrix stiffness regulates macrophage polarization and tumor intravasation.
  • Mechanobiology-driven control of immune-cell metabolism and function.
